The Accounting Close Process

As you know, the purpose of the Retained Earnings account is to show all of the net income that a business has earned over its life, excluding any income that has been distributed to shareholders. What we haven’t yet discussed, however, is how any amounts actually get into the Retained Earnings account.

In addition, you know that the point of using revenue and expense accounts is to track the operations of a business over a period of time. But we have not yet discussed how, for example, this year’s rent expense is separated from last year’s rent expense, given that both are recorded in the same account.

The accounting close process is the answer to both of these questions.
