Topic #156: From the assets side: Working capital (WC) = Current Assets (CA) - Current Liabilities (CL) The definition of WC is current assets less current liabilities.

From the assets side:

Working capital (WC) = Current Assets (CA) - Current Liabilities (CL)

The definition of WC is current assets less current liabilities.

Topic #157: This figure is a measure of liquidity. We can consider liquidity as an indicator of cash availability. It is clearly not the same thing as wealth: many people and companies . (top)

This figure is a measure of liquidity. We can consider liquidity as an indicator of cash availability. It is clearly not the same thing as wealth: many people and companies who are very wealthy do not have a high degree of liquidity. This happens if the wealth is tied up in assets that are not easily converted into cash. For instance, large farm and plantation owners have lots of assets, but may have difficulty in meeting day-to-day cash demands – they have much wealth but they are not liquid. This can be true for companies also. It is not sufficient to have assets; it is necessary to ensure that there is sufficient liquidity to meet ongoing cash needs.
A most important value, Working capital (WC) represents the amount of day-to-day operating liquidity available to a business.
Operating liquidity is a term used to describe cash and near-cash assets available to meet ongoing cash needs.
A company can be very rich in assets, but short of liquidity if these assets cannot readily be converted into cash.

Q: What is liquidity an indicator of? A: Cash availability

Q: Is liquidity the same as wealth? A: No, they are not the same thing

Q: Why might a wealthy person or company have low liquidity? A: If their wealth is tied up in assets that are not easily converted into cash

Q: What is an example of a wealthy entity that might have low liquidity? A: Large farm and plantation owners

Q: What does Working Capital (WC) represent? A: The amount of day-to-day operating liquidity available to a business

Q: What is operating liquidity? A: Cash and near-cash assets available to meet ongoing cash needs

Q: Can a company be rich in assets but short on liquidity? A: Yes, if these assets cannot be readily converted into cash

Q: Why is liquidity important for businesses? A: To meet day-to-day cash demands and ensure sufficient cash for ongoing needs

Q: What's the relationship between assets and liquidity? A: Having assets is not sufficient; it's necessary to ensure there's enough liquidity to meet ongoing cash needs

Q: Why is Working Capital described as a "most important value"? A: Because it represents the amount of day-to-day operating liquidity available to a business
