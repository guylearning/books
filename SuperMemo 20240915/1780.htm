    Item #1,770: Why are vector images better than raster images for scaling? (top)

    Why are vector images better than raster images for scaling?
    No loss of quality at any size.

    Vector images use mathematical equations to represent graphics, allowing infinite scaling without losing clarity, unlike raster images which pixelate when enlarged.

    Why are vector image files smaller than raster image files?
    Vector image files only need to hold a handful of algorithms, rather than information on every pixel in the image individually.
